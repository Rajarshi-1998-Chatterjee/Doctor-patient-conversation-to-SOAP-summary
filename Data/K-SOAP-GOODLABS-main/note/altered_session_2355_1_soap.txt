Assessment
35-year-old male with a history of eczema presenting with an erythematous rash with itching on arms and neck. Likely a flare-up of eczema triggered by an allergen or stress. No signs of systemic illness or infection.
Diagnosis: Eczema flare-up.

Plan
Eczema flare-up:
1. Prescribe a stronger topical steroid than over-the-counter hydrocortisone cream.
2. Start a non-sedating antihistamine to control the itch.
3. Order blood work to check for any underlying issues.
4. Refer to a dermatologist for follow-up.
5. Advise to keep the affected area moisturized and avoid hot showers.
6. Counsel to avoid scratching to prevent infection and to watch for signs of infection such as increased redness, swelling, or pus.
7. Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess response to treatment.
8. Instruct patient to call the office with any questions or concerns before the follow-up.
9. Advise to seek immediate medical attention if signs of infection develop.
