Assessment
62-year-old female with a history of high cholesterol and hypothyroidism, presenting with a one-month history of an enlarging, itchy red patch on the forearm. Given the patient's family history of psoriasis and eczema and the nature of the symptoms, a preliminary diagnosis of eczema is considered.
Diagnosis: Eczema.

Plan
Eczema:
1. Prescribe topical corticosteroid cream for inflammation and itching.
2. Order blood work for thyroid function and complete blood count.
3. Consider skin biopsy if no improvement.
4. Advise use of hypoallergenic products to avoid irritation.
5. Schedule follow-up in four weeks.
6. Instruct patient to visit the emergency room for any worsening symptoms or new concerns.
